How Did I End Up Here?

It all started back in 2014. I got my first touchscreen phone. I was 10, excited beyond reason, and of course the first thing I did was try to install a game. All my friends were playing Dream League Soccer 2014 and I wanted in.

Tried installing from Google Play — boom, out of space. My Sony Xperia Tipo Dual only had 4GB total. I read online that I needed to manually download the OBB cache and unpack it into /Android/obb. Sounded like magic. How could it work? But after a week of hesitation, I downloaded the cache, used zArchiver, unpacked it... and bam — it worked!

That’s how I discovered the file system. I realized that I knew absolutely nothing, classic Socratic moment.

The issue was that Play Market needed enough space to download *and* unpack the cache, but it didn’t delete the original file during unpacking. So I needed ~2.5x more space than the actual archive size. Simple, but game-changing.

A year later I managed to install CWM recovery and upgrade my phone from Android 4.0.4 to 4.4.0, even though it wasn’t officially supported. That pulled me into the world of custom ROMs and kernels. I was chasing maximum compatibility between old hardware and new games.

Around 2016 my parents banned me from using the laptop. I was ignoring chores. They took the charger. Easy fix, my friend had the same charger and a broken laptop. I kept going, did my chores, hid the charger, and was back online when they were out. Until one day they got suspicious and set a password.

I stared at that lock screen for two weeks, dreaming of bypassing it. And I did, on my second try. First attempt? Dusting the keyboard with flour and asking my mom to enter the code “just in case” I looked away. Honest.

But they kept changing the password. So I prepped a USB during allowed hours, loaded a script, and created a new user via command line. Everyone was happy. I was back online, they thought I wasn’t.

Then came WiFi hacking with wifite2 and kali-nethunter, capturing handshakes, and sniffing passwords from HTTP Facebook logins using interceptor-ng.

So began my journey…
